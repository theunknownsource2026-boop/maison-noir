001 / Origin

Born from refusal.

Maison Noir was founded in 2024 by two people who had spent a decade in the fashion industry and hated almost everything about it. The waste. The speed. The disposability dressed up as desire.

We started with a single question: what if a fashion house made only what it was proud of? No filler. No commercial concessions. No pieces that exist just to hit a price point.

The answer was twelve pieces a year. Each one developed obsessively, produced in limited runs, and released without announcement. No lookbook dates, no press previews, no pre-orders. When it's ready, it appears.

002 / Timeline

How we got here.

2024

Founded in Paris

First atelier opens at 13 Rue de Braque, 3rd arrondissement. Three pieces released in the first year. All sold within 72 hours.

2024

The Architect Coat

Our first signature piece debuts. Hand-cut from Italian double-faced wool. Featured in Vogue France without a press release — they found us.

2025

Dead Stock Initiative

We commit to sourcing 70% of fabrics from dead stock and single-origin mills. Zero new synthetic textiles.

2025

Tokyo & New York

Private showrooms open by appointment only. No retail presence. No wholesale. Direct relationship with every client.

2026

SS26: Concrete Elegance

Our most ambitious editorial yet. Shot across three countries in abandoned brutalist architecture. Twelve new pieces join the archive.

003 / Vision

The people behind the silence.

"Fashion has a noise problem. Every brand screaming for attention has forgotten that the most powerful statement in a room is the person who doesn't need to speak."

Our founding team brings together expertise from Celine, The Row, and Rick Owens — houses that understand the weight of restraint. Every design decision at Maison Noir passes through a single filter: would we wear this ourselves, every day, for years?

If the answer isn't an immediate yes, the piece doesn't exist.

004 / Craft

One atelier. One standard.

01

Sourcing

We travel to mills personally. Every fabric is touched, draped, and tested before a single meter is purchased. We work with three mills — two in Italy, one in Japan.

02

Pattern & Cut

Every pattern is draped directly on the form. No CAD. No digital shortcuts. The fabric decides the silhouette. We average 14 iterations per piece before final approval.

03

Construction

One maker handles each piece from first cut to final press. No assembly line. No handoffs. Each coat takes 40+ hours. Each dress, 25. The Monolith Trench, 60.

04

Finishing

Hand-pressed seams. Individually numbered labels. Each piece ships in a custom garment bag with a certificate of authenticity and care instructions written for that specific fabric.

005 / Values

What we believe.

Permanence over trend

We design for the next decade, not the next season. If it won't be relevant in ten years, we don't make it.

Scarcity over scale

Limited runs aren't a marketing tactic. They're the natural result of making things by hand, slowly, with care.

Material over marketing

We spend more on fabric than advertising. The product is the message. Everything else is noise.

Silence over spectacle

No fashion week. No influencer campaigns. No runway shows. We'd rather you discover us through the person standing next to you.
